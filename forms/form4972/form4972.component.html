Form 4972

(2025)

Tax on Lump-Sum Distributions

Department of the Treasury — Internal Revenue Service

Use this form to figure the tax on a qualified lump-sum distribution using the 20% capital gain election, the 10-year tax option, or both. For participants born before January 2, 1936.

Part I — General Information

Part II — Computation of Tax

1. Total lump-sum distribution
2. Capital gain portion (20% capital gain election)
3. Ordinary income portion
4. Total taxable amount (line 1 minus line 2)
5. Participant's birth date
6. Number of years of plan participation

Part III — 10-Year Tax Option

7. Ordinary income (10-year averaging)
8. Multiply line 7 by 10% (0.10)
9. Tax on line 8 using 2024 tax rates
10. Tax on lump-sum (10-year option)

Part IV — 20% Capital Gain Election

11. Capital gain portion
12. Multiply line 11 by 20% (0.20)
13. Tax on ordinary income (from line 10)
14. Total tax (line 12 plus line 13)

Part V — Election

I understand that this election is irrevocable and applies to all of the lump-sum distribution.
