Form 8995

(2024)

Qualified Business Income Deduction

Department of the Treasury — Internal Revenue Service

Use this form to figure your qualified business income (QBI) deduction. For individuals, estates, and trusts with QBI from qualified trades or businesses.

Part I — General Information

Part II — QBI Computation for Each Trade or Business

1. Qualified business income (QBI)
2. W-2 wages paid by qualified trade or business
3. Unadjusted basis of qualified property (UBIA)
4. Taxable income before QBI deduction
5. Net capital gain

Part III — Threshold and Phase-In

6. Threshold amount (single: $170,050; MFJ: $340,100 for 2024)
7. Enter taxable income (line 4)
8. Is line 7 more than line 6?
9. If no, Phase-in range (line 6 + $50,000 for single, +$100,000 for MFJ)

Part IV — Deduction Calculation

10. If line 8 is "No," enter 20% of QBI (lesser of line 1 × 20% or 50% of W-2 wages)
11. If line 8 is "Yes," enter greater of: (a) 50% of W-2 wages, or (b) 25% of W-2 wages + 2.5% of UBIA
12. Enter 20% of taxable income minus net capital gain
13. QBI deduction (lesser of line 10, 11, or 12)

Part V — Multiple Trades or Businesses

14. Total QBI from all trades (sum of all Part II, line 1)
15. Total W-2 wages (sum of all Part II, line 2)
16. Total UBIA (sum of all Part II, line 3)
17. Total taxable income (line 4)
18. Net capital gain
19. Total QBI deduction (sum of all Part IV, line 13)

Part VI — Limitations

20. Form W-2 wages from employer
21. Taxable income from Form 1040, line 11
22. Net capital gain (from Schedule D, if any)
23. Qualified business income deduction
24. Enter amount from line 19 or line 13 (whichever applies)
